Resonance 共振

The ILLUMANATE Sustainable Living Building Challenge, initiated by the Living Future Institute of Australia, is a heritage building retrofit design competition. At its core is the Living Building Challenge (LBC), a certification program, advocacy tool, and philosophy that sets the highest standards for sustainability in the built environment. The LBC is organized into seven key performance areas: Place, Water, Energy, Health and Happiness, Materials, Equity, and Beauty.

ILLUMANATE可持续生活建筑挑战竞赛是Living Future Institute of Australia主要发起的遗产建筑改造设计竞赛。竞赛的主旨是Living Building Challenge，简称 LBC。这是一个认证项目、倡导工具和哲学，定义了当今建筑环境中最先进的可持续性标准。可持续生活建筑挑战分为七个表现领域：地点、水、能源、健康与幸福、材料、公平和美学。

The design of Resonance primarily focuses on the adaptive reuse of a heritage building located in Sunshine North, Melbourne. The project aims to transform the lower-value areas of the building's interior while preserving its heritage elements, and to reshape public social spaces that are needed by the surrounding community. The design takes into account the diverse cultural groups in Australia, including local Aboriginal communities and their unique cultural elements, with the goal of creating an inclusive and welcoming architectural space for all, fostering cultural exchange and community integration.

"共振"（Resonance）的设计主要目标是对位于墨尔本阳光北区的遗产建筑进行适应性再利用。设计将依据遗产建筑评估标准，对低价值部分进行室内改造，同时重塑周边社区所需的公共社交空间。方案充分考虑了澳大利亚多元化的人群，包括当地的原住民及其独特的文化元素，旨在打造一个开放包容的建筑空间，服务于所有人群，促进文化交流与社区融合。

Breathable Interior Spaces 可呼吸的室内空间

The original building, completed in 1972, is designed in the Brutalist style, characterized by clean horizontal lines and dominant board-marked off-form concrete, giving it a strong sense of order. The focus of this design is the transformation and revitalization of the building's interior spaces. We aim to repurpose the structure into a multifunctional community hub, incorporating a range of spaces for childcare, commercial, educational, office, and creative purposes. This approach not only addresses the diverse needs of the community but also preserves and reuses the building's original structure and spatial value. To address the lighting challenges within the interior, we have introduced a series of engaging skylights into the roof and partially removed floor slabs to create double-height spaces. This allows natural light, fresh air, and greenery to penetrate the building, enhancing the connection between indoor and outdoor environments. 原建筑建于1972年，采用了粗野主义风格，以干净的水平线条和板状脱模混凝土建造，呈现出强烈的秩序感。此次设计的核心是对建筑内部空间的改造与活化。我们将其转变为一个多功能的社区空间，涵盖托儿所、商业、教育、办公、创意等多维度的功能，以满足不同社区需求的同时，保留并再利用建筑的原始结构与空间价值。为解决室内的照明问题，我们在屋顶引入大量有趣的天窗，并局部去掉楼板创造通高空间，将阳光、风雨、绿意引入室内。

Boxes of Light 光之盒-公共空间

We have integrated a variety of glass boxes, focused on light, into the building's roof. These glass structures create vertical passages that guide natural light into the previously dark ground floor. At the same time, they seamlessly connect the activities on the first floor with those on the second floor. The interplay of light and space enhances the spatial experience, infusing the interior with warmth and vitality. 我们在建筑屋顶上设计并融入了多种形式的光线玻璃盒子，这些玻璃盒子塑造了垂直的通道，将自然光引入原本昏暗的首层。与此同时，它们也巧妙地将首层与二层的活动无缝衔接。光与空间的交织，不仅赋予室内温暖与活力，还提升了空间体验感。

Resonance of the Old and New 新旧共振

In the design, we respect the original building structure, retaining the concrete formwork columns and horizontal textures. The ceiling is designed in a circular shape around these structural columns, expanding outward like ripples on water, imbuing the building with renewed vitality. The floor features a simple terrazzo finish, and circular partitions accentuate the dynamic circular forms, further enhancing the overall design language. 在设计中，我们尊重原有的建筑结构，保留了原始混凝土支模形成的圆柱与水平纹理。室内吊顶则围绕这些结构柱设计成环形，层层向外扩展，仿佛水波纹般扩散开来，赋予建筑重新焕发的生命力。地面采用了简约的水磨石处理，并通过环形分隔条强化了动感的圆形元素，进一步呼应整体的设计语言。
