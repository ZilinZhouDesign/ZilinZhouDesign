Origami 折纸

Origami is an art form that transforms paper into intricate shapes through precise calculations of angles and folding techniques. It is not only artistic but also involves mathematical and geometric principles, allowing for the creation of highly detailed designs. Originating in Japan, origami has been widely adopted around the world. Renowned Japanese designer Issey Miyake has created numerous art pieces inspired by origami, incorporating its elements into fashion and architecture, showcasing the endless possibilities of origami in contemporary art. Combining science and art, origami's folds are carefully calculated, reflecting the aesthetic of geometric forms and spatial structures.

折纸是一种通过精确计算角度和折叠技巧，将纸张转变为各种复杂形态的艺术形式。它不仅具有艺术性，还涉及到数学和几何原理，理论上可以折叠出各种精细的设计。折纸的起源可以追溯到日本，并在全球范围内得到了广泛应用。著名日本设计师三宅一生便创作了许多与折纸相关的艺术作品，他将折纸的元素融入时尚和建筑设计中，展现了折纸在现代艺术中的无限可能性。折纸结合了科学与艺术，它的每一次折叠都经过精确的计算，反映出几何形态和空间结构的美学。

Research Project 研究课题

Crease Design 折痕设计

Typically, creases in origami are classified into two types: mountain folds and valley folds. A mountain crease occurs when the fold creates a peak, with the crease line on top, while a valley crease creates a depression, with the crease line on the bottom. By calculating the folding angles, one can generate a crease pattern and the corresponding flat-folded diagram. However, the charm of origami lies in the free transformation of shapes after folding, creating an aesthetic effect of rotating light and shadows in three-dimensional space. 常规来说，折痕会分两种：山折与谷折。折痕居上形成凸起,即为山线折痕(mountain crease),折痕居下形成凹陷,即为谷线折痕(valley crease)。通过计算折叠角度，可以计算出折痕图与对应的平折效果示意图。但折纸的魅力就在于折叠后的形态的自由变化，在三维空间创造出转动光影的美学效果。。

Icarus's Wings 伊卡洛斯之翼

Icarus, a figure from Greek mythology, used wings designed by his father Daedalus to fly toward the sun. However, he flew too high, causing the sun to melt the wax holding the wings together, and ultimately fell into the sea. This story inspired the design of Icarus' wings, using origami elements to create the wing structure. By carefully calculating folds and angles, the wings were designed with a streamlined shape and smooth shoulder transitions. The twisting angles simulate the dynamic movement of the wings in flight, embodying the power and freedom of flight while creating an aesthetic interplay of light and shadow. 伊卡洛斯是希腊神话中的人物，他用父亲代达罗斯设计的羽翼飞向太阳，但因飞得太高，太阳融化了翅膀的蜡，最终坠海。这个故事启发了我设计伊卡洛斯之翼，运用折纸元素构建羽翼的结构，精确计算折痕和角度，设计出流线型的翅膀和肩部过渡。通过巧妙的扭转角度，模拟翅膀在飞行中的动态变化，展现飞行的力量与自由，同时创造出光影交织的美学效果。

Origami Patterns 折纸图纸

To achieve a dynamic and flexible structure, every part of the origami design has been meticulously crafted to ensure the wings maintain a sense of lightness during movement. The geometric forms of the origami allow the wings to effectively support weight, ensuring they are not too heavy or awkward when worn. During the design process, the folds and layering techniques of origami were used to express the dynamic transformation of the wings—from unfolding to folding—creating different visual effects with each fold. The design of a single wing serves as a profound reflection on the story of Icarus, subtly alluding to the melting of the wings. 为了实现这种动态和灵活的结构，折纸的每一部分都经过了严格的设计，确保了翅膀在移动时能够呈现出轻盈的感觉，同时折纸的几何形式使得翅膀能够有效承载重量，让翅膀在背负时不会过于沉重或笨拙。在设计过程中，利用折纸的折痕和层次效果，表现出翅膀的动态变化——从展开到收拢，每一次折叠都创造了不同的视觉效果。单边翅膀的设计则是对伊卡洛斯故事的深刻反思，通过单边翅膀暗示羽翼的融化。
