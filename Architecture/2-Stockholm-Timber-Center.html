    • Stockholm Timber Center

    This project represents a harmonious blend of urbanity and a sense of place, an exploration into the rejuvenation of context and the art of creating distinctive spaces.

    The project revolves around the symbiotic relationship between urban life and architectural design. The historic town is interwoven with a network of streets, varying in scale and direction, which intricately divides the island into numerous smaller parcels. This intricate and organic urban fabric seamlessly extends into the architectural framework.

    Location

    Gamla Stan, Stockholm

    Program

    Timber Centre

    Phase

    Competition

    Texture Restructured

    Inspired by the Stockholm Street Mapping at the Sweden Expo Pavilion, the existing urban fabric within the site has been reimagined on a smaller scale, cultivating a vibrant street ambiance within the architectural structure itself.

    Simultaneously, drawing inspiration from Stockholm's unique tradition of exploring rooftops on foot, the roof design has been elevated to a novel prototype. The diverse rooflines reveal two distinct design systems that engage with both the external streetscape and the internal architectural space. These innovative design choices promise visitors a distinctive experience, bridging the gap between the past and the present within the heart of the timber center.

    Dynamic Enclosure

    The building features multiple accessible entrances, each connecting to city corners, green spaces, and squares. Its folding roof exudes dynamism, enclosing into a circle, drawing people towards the center. The rhythmic wooden frame facade endows the building with a lively posture. Transparency characterizes both its interior and exterior, resembling a semi-transparent box integrated into the urban fabric, juxtaposed with the surrounding sturdy structures.

    Wood Construction

    The construction concept revolves around a steel and timber framework, aligning with the idea of utilizing wood in a recyclable manner and ensuring the overall building's disassembly capability. The timber elements are all of small dimensions, facilitating local processing, even within the confines of this timber workshop. The roof comprises a fully wooden structure supported by timber trusses, which in turn determine the angles of the folded surfaces, creating a rhythmic roofline.
