    • Seam Bridge

    "Seam: Natural Symbiosis" is a conceptual bridge design that integrates nature and architecture, exuding a unique aesthetic and vitality. Inspired by the rugged stone bridges of Moganshan, the design preserves graceful curves while employing lightweight steel structures in place of traditional stone arches, thus creating an elegant and airy visual effect.

    The seams between the bridge deck and the bridge body create a sense of natural breathing, making the interaction between humans and nature more intuitive. The bridge is disassembled into different geometric volumes, the bridge body, guardrail, and handrail are separated, allowing people to rethink the meaning of connection. Through this design, the observer can appreciate the details of the bridge at close range and blend with it.

    Form and Nature

    Grass grows from the gaps in the bridge, and rainwater flows off the bridge deck, making the bridge not only a river crossing but also an existence intertwined with natural life. Gaps are left between the railing and the ground pavement of the bridge, which not only facilitates drainage but also enhances the design sense of the bridge deck's volume, allowing the bridge deck, body, and handrails to separate lightly from each other.

    Night View

    At night, LED light strips are hidden under the handrails to provide illumination for the bridge. The slightly yellow light is reflected by the aluminum panel surface of the guardrail and falls on the creek through the gaps in the bridge, bringing starlight to the water surface. This concealed design adds a sense of mystery and charm to the entire bridge.

    Structural Optimization

    In the initial design phase, Kangaroo dynamic simulation plug-ins were used to optimize the bridge's shape to find the best arch shape to maintain the simplicity and beauty of the shell's shape while ensuring more reasonable stress. Later, Karamba finite element analysis was used to constantly adjust the shell curve to ensure the structural strength requirements.

    During construction, prefabricated steel beams, cold-formed anodized aluminum plates, and other assembly construction technologies can be used to reduce on-site construction time and costs while minimizing environmental damage, demonstrating concern and respect for environmental protection.
